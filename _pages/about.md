I am currently a first-year physics PhD student and Hughes Fellow at WashU. My research focuses on two distinct areas of physics: the anatomy of fractional quantum hall (FQH) ground states in condensed matter systems and the astrophysical investigation on the missing red giants near the galactic center. I graduated cum laude from Princeton University with a B.A. in astrophysical sciences and a minor in mathematics.

Research Interests
======
I'm always interested in using mathematical tools to describe physical phenomena. Currently, I am going through [Kong & Zhang (2022)]([url](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.05565)) to understand how category theory can be applied to the study of gapped quantum liquids without symmetry, in addition to more general condensed matter phenomena. My main research intersts are the classification of FQH states projected to the lowest Landau level (LLL) on various geometries and astrophysical interactions between red giants and the galactic center populations.
