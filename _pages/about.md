I am currently a first-year physics PhD student and Hughes Fellow at WashU. My research focuses on two distinct areas of physics: the small-scale anatomy of fractional quantum hall (FQH) states in condensed matter systems and the large-scale astrophysical investigation on the missing red giants near the galactic center. I graduated cum laude from Princeton University with a B.A. in astrophysical sciences and a minor in mathematics.

Research Interests
======
I'm interested in using mathematical tools to describe physical phenomena. Currently, I am going through [Kong & Zhang (2022)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.05565) to understand how category theory can be applied to the study of gapped quantum liquids without symmetry, in addition to more general condensed matter phenomena. My current research interests are as follows: 

* Classifying FQH states projected to the lowest Landau level (LLL) on various geometries
* Quantifying the functional lifetime of the galactic center red giants by comparing mean exit times induced by Sgr A* tidal disruption with time-evolving relaxation diffusion coefficients in discretized, collision-dependent Monte-Carlo simulations
